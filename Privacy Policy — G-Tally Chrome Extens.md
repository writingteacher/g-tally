# Privacy Policy — G-Tally Chrome Extension

**Effective date:** March 1, 2026  
**Last updated:** March 1, 2026  
**Applies to:** G-Tally Chrome Extension v1.0


## Plain English summary

G-Tally does not collect, store, transmit, or share any data about you or your calendar. It runs entirely inside your browser. Nothing leaves your device.


## 1. Who we are

G-Tally is a Chrome browser extension developed by **Rob Whyte**, an independent developer and technical writer based in South Korea. If you have questions about this policy, contact: gtally.studio@gmail.com


## 2. What data G-Tally accesses

G-Tally reads the visible content of your Google Calendar page. Specifically the text content of event blocks currently rendered in your browser. This includes:

- Event titles as they appear on screen
- Start and end times displayed on event blocks
- The colours assigned to event blocks

This data is read directly from your browser's display and is used only to calculate time totals shown in the widget. It is never stored, exported, or transmitted in any form.

> **G-Tally does not access your Google account, Google Calendar API, or any Google services.** It reads only what is already visible on your screen.


## 3. What data G-Tally does not collect

G-Tally does not collect, store, transmit, or share:

- Your name, email address, or any personal identifiers
- Your Google account credentials or authentication tokens
- The content of your calendar events beyond what is visible on screen
- Your location, IP address, or device information
- Usage statistics, analytics, or behavioural data
- Cookies or any persistent identifiers
- Any data from other websites or browser tabs


## 4. Data storage

G-Tally stores no data of any kind. All time calculations are performed in memory while the Google Calendar tab is open and are discarded when the tab is closed or refreshed. There is no database, no local storage, and no session storage used by this extension.


## 5. Data sharing

G-Tally does not share data with any third parties because it does not collect any data to share. There are no advertising networks, analytics providers, or data brokers involved in the operation of this extension.


## 6. Network requests

G-Tally makes no network requests. It does not connect to any external servers, APIs, or services. The extension operates entirely within your browser with no outbound communication of any kind.


## 7. Permissions

G-Tally requests the following Chrome permissions:

| Permission | Reason |
|---|---|
| `activeTab` | Allows the extension to interact with the currently active Google Calendar tab only. Does not grant access to other tabs. |
| `storage` | G-Tally does not currently use Chrome storage to save any data. |
| `host_permissions: calendar.google.com` | Restricts the extension to run only on Google Calendar pages. G-Tally cannot access any other websites. |


## 8. Children's privacy

G-Tally does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children under the age of 13. As the extension collects no data whatsoever, it presents no privacy risk to users of any age.


## 9. Changes to this policy

If this privacy policy changes, the updated version will be published in this repository and the "Last updated" date at the top of this document will be revised. Continued use of G-Tally after a policy update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

Given that G-Tally collects no data, material changes to this policy are unlikely.


## 10. Your rights

As G-Tally collects no personal data, there is no data to access, correct, export, or delete. If you wish to stop using G-Tally entirely, remove it from Chrome via `chrome://extensions`.
